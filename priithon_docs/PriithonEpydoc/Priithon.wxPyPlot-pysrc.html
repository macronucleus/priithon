  15  """ 
  16  This is a simple light weight plotting module that can be used with Boa or 
  17  easily integrated into your own wxPython application.  The emphasis is on small 
  18  size and fast plotting for large data sets.  It has a reasonable number of 
  19  features to do line and scatter graphs easily.  It is not as sophisticated or as 
  20  powerful as SciPy Plt or Chaco.  Both of these are great packages but consume 
  21  huge amounts of computer resources for simple plots.  They can be found at 
  22  http://scipy.com 
  23   
  24  This file contains two parts; first the re-usable library stuff, then, after 
  25  a "if __name__=='__main__'" test, a simple frame and a few default plots 
  26  for examples and testing. 
  27   
  28  Based on wxPlotCanvas 
  29  Written by K.Hinsen, R. Srinivasan; 
  30  Ported to wxPython Harm van der Heijden, feb 1999 
  31   
  32  Major Additions Gordon Williams Feb. 2003 (g_will@cyberus.ca) 
  33      -More style options 
  34      -Zooming using mouse "rubber band" 
  35      -Scroll left, right 
  36      -Grid(graticule) 
  37      -Printing, preview, and page set up (margins) 
  38      -Axis and title labels 
  39      -Cursor xy axis values 
  40      -Doc strings and lots of comments 
  41      -Optimizations for large number of points 
  42      -Legends 
  43       
  44  Did a lot of work here to speed markers up. Only a factor of 4 improvement 
  45  though. Lines are much faster than markers, especially filled markers.  Stay 
  46  away from circles and triangles unless you only have a few thousand points. 
  47   
  48  Times for 25,000 points 
  49  Line - 0.078 sec 
  50  Markers 
  51  Square -                   0.22 sec 
  52  dot -                      0.10 
  53  circle -                   0.87 
  54  cross,plus -               0.28 
  55  triangle, triangle_down -  0.90 
  56   
  57  Thanks to Chris Barker for getting this version working on Linux. 
  58   
  59  Zooming controls with mouse (when enabled): 
  60      Left mouse drag - Zoom box. 
  61      Left mouse double click - reset zoom. 
  62      Right mouse click - zoom out centred on click location.
